## Overview\n[[Information Management]] is the cycle of organizational activity: the acquisition of information from one or more sources, the custodianship and the distribution of that information to those who need it, and its ultimate disposition through archiving or deletion. Effective management ensures that data remains useful and accessible while minimizing waste.\n\n## Data Quality and Redundancy\nA significant challenge in [[Information Management]] is the proliferation of [[Duplicate Content]]. When the same information exists in multiple locations without synchronization, it leads to [[Content Redundancy]]. This redundancy can obscure the "single source of truth," making it difficult for users to identify the most accurate or up-to-date version of a document. Managing these overlaps is crucial for maintaining system integrity and storage efficiency.\n\n## Development and Testing\nDuring the design phase of information systems, developers often use [[Dummy Content]] to simulate real-world data environments. This may include [[Placeholder Text]] to visualize layouts or a [[Test Note]] to verify system functionality. Proper [[Information Management]] protocols dictate that these temporary elements should be removed or replaced before a system moves into production to prevent confusion with actual operational data.
